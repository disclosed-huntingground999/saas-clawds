# 12 — Revenue

> Building sustainable, growing revenue — not just acquiring customers, but maximizing lifetime value.

Revenue is the lifeblood of your SaaS. This department covers how you structure billing, expand revenue from existing customers, and build the financial engine that makes your business sustainable. The goal isn't just to charge money — it's to align your pricing and packaging with the value customers receive so that revenue grows naturally as customers succeed.

## Why This Matters

SaaS economics are simple: grow MRR, reduce churn, expand accounts. The companies that win aren't always the ones with the most customers — they're the ones that extract the most value per customer through smart packaging, expansion revenue, and enterprise deals.

## How to Work Through This Department

1. **Design your subscription model** — this is the foundation everything else builds on
2. **Define upsell triggers** — know exactly when and how to nudge users to upgrade
3. **Introduce annual plans** — improve cash flow and reduce churn simultaneously
4. **Plan add-ons strategically** — create additional revenue without bloating your core product
5. **Prepare for enterprise** — when inbound interest from larger companies arrives, be ready

## Key Metrics to Track

- **MRR / ARR** — Monthly and Annual Recurring Revenue
- **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** — target >100%, meaning existing customers grow revenue even after churn (best-in-class: 120%+)
- **Expansion MRR** — revenue from upsells, cross-sells, and add-ons
- **ARPU** — Average Revenue Per User, trending up over time
- **LTV:CAC ratio** — Lifetime Value to Customer Acquisition Cost (target: >3:1)
- **Annual plan adoption** — percentage of customers on annual vs. monthly
