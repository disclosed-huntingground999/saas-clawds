# 01 — Idea: The Foundation of Your SaaS

> Every great SaaS company starts with a problem worth solving. This department is where you crystallize **what** you're building and **why** it matters.

## Purpose

The Idea phase is the bedrock of your entire SaaS business. Skip this, and you'll spend months building something nobody wants. Do this well, and every decision downstream — from your MVP scope to your pricing — becomes clearer.

This department walks you through five critical exercises:

| Subfolder | What You'll Produce | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| [problem-discovery](./problem-discovery/) | A validated problem statement with evidence | P0 |
| [market-research](./market-research/) | Market sizing, trends, and dynamics analysis | P0 |
| [niche-selection](./niche-selection/) | A scored niche decision with rationale | P0 |
| [competitor-analysis](./competitor-analysis/) | Competitive landscape map with gaps identified | P1 |
| [opportunity-mapping](./opportunity-mapping/) | A synthesized opportunity canvas | P1 |

## How to Work Through This Department

**Order matters.** Work through these subfolders sequentially:

1. **Problem Discovery** — Identify and document the core problem
2. **Market Research** — Understand the size and shape of the market
3. **Niche Selection** — Pick your beachhead segment
4. **Competitor Analysis** — Map who else is in this space
5. **Opportunity Mapping** — Synthesize everything into a clear opportunity thesis

## Time Estimate

| Approach | Time |
|---|---|
| Quick pass (solo founder, existing domain knowledge) | 3–5 days |
| Thorough pass (new domain, primary research) | 2–4 weeks |

## Key Principle

**Specificity beats ambition.** "We help mid-market e-commerce brands reduce cart abandonment by 30%" is infinitely more useful than "We're building an AI platform for businesses."

## What Success Looks Like

When you finish this department, you should be able to articulate:
- The **exact problem** you're solving
- **Who** has this problem (specific persona)
- **How big** the opportunity is (with numbers)
- **Why now** is the right time to build this
- **What gap** exists that you can fill
