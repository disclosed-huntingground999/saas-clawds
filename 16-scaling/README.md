# 16 — Scaling

> Transitioning from startup to scale-up — building the machine that builds the company.

Scaling is about building the organizational infrastructure to grow from 10 to 100 to 1,000+ and beyond. In the early days, you can hold everything in your head. But at scale, you need systems, processes, automation, and the right people to keep the machine running. This is where you stop being a scrappy startup and start being a real company.

The best founders know that scaling isn't just about growing revenue — it's about growing the organization's capacity to handle that revenue without breaking.

## Why This Matters

Most SaaS companies don't die from lack of customers — they die from inability to scale. The product breaks under load. The team burns out. Processes that worked for 10 customers collapse at 1,000. Support response times crater. Bugs ship faster than fixes. Scaling solves these problems by building repeatable systems before they become emergencies.

## How to Work Through This Department

1. **Document your systems** — write down what's in your head before you forget
2. **Automate what's repeatable** — stop doing manually what a script can handle
3. **Plan your first hires** — know who you need and when
4. **Think about expansion** — when the domestic market is working, go international
5. **Define the endgame** — know where you're heading so every decision points there

## Key Metrics to Track

- **Revenue per Employee** — efficiency of the organization (target: $200K+ for SaaS)
- **Burn Multiple** — net burn / net new ARR (target: <2x)
- **Magic Number** — net new ARR / prior quarter S&M spend (target: >0.75)
- **Time to Hire** — days from opening a role to accepted offer
- **Process Coverage** — percentage of core processes with documented SOPs
- **Automation Rate** — percentage of tasks handled without manual intervention
