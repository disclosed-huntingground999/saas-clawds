# 18 — Finance & Unit Economics: Run the Numbers

> SaaS is a numbers game. This department covers financial modeling, unit economics, bookkeeping, billing operations, and fundraising — so you know your burn rate, runway, LTV:CAC, and when (or whether) to raise.

## Purpose

Founders who understand their numbers make better decisions: when to hire, when to cut, when to raise, when to pivot. This department gives you the structure to track revenue, costs, unit economics, and investor-ready financials.

| Subfolder | What You'll Produce | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| [financial-modeling](./financial-modeling/) | Revenue projections, burn, runway, scenarios | P0 |
| [unit-economics](./unit-economics/) | LTV, CAC, payback period, margin analysis | P0 |
| [bookkeeping](./bookkeeping/) | Chart of accounts, monthly close, tax prep | P1 |
| [billing-operations](./billing-operations/) | Payments, invoicing, dunning, refunds | P1 |
| [fundraising](./fundraising/) | Pitch deck, investor pipeline, cap table, term sheets | P1 |

## How to Work Through This Department

1. **Financial Modeling** — Project revenue and expenses; model scenarios
2. **Unit Economics** — Calculate LTV, CAC, payback; set targets
3. **Bookkeeping** — Set up accounting, monthly close, tax readiness
4. **Billing Operations** — Align with [12-revenue] for invoicing and collections
5. **Fundraising** — If raising: deck, pipeline, cap table, term sheets

## Time Estimate

| Approach | Time |
|---|---|
| Bootstrap (spreadsheets + Stripe + accountant) | 1–2 weeks |
| With fractional CFO or accounting firm | 2–4 weeks |

## Key Principle

**Model early, track often.** A simple model beats no model. Update monthly. Your unit economics (LTV:CAC > 3, payback < 18 months for SMB) drive everything.
