# 15 — Growth

> Compounding growth — moving beyond linear acquisition to build growth flywheels that accelerate over time.

Growth is about building systems where each new user increases the likelihood of the next user joining. Linear growth (spend $1, get 1 user) is fragile and expensive. Compounding growth (each user brings 0.3 more users) creates a flywheel that accelerates over time. This department covers the mechanisms that make your growth self-reinforcing.

The best SaaS companies don't just grow — they grow faster as they get bigger. That's the power of referral loops, community effects, product-led virality, and strategic expansion.

## Why This Matters

Paid acquisition gets more expensive every year. CAC across every channel is rising. The companies that win long-term are the ones that build organic growth engines: referrals, community, virality, and product-led loops that bring in customers at near-zero marginal cost. Growth engineering is how you escape the paid acquisition treadmill.

## How to Work Through This Department

1. **Audit PLG opportunities** — the product is your best growth channel
2. **Design referral programs** — incentivize users to invite others
3. **Build viral loops** — make sharing a natural part of using the product
4. **Invest in community** — create a gathering place for your users
5. **Plan expansion** — once the core market is won, expand strategically

## Key Metrics to Track

- **Viral Coefficient (K-factor)** — average invites per user × conversion rate of invites
- **Viral Cycle Time** — how long it takes for one viral loop to complete
- **Referral Rate** — percentage of customers who refer at least one person
- **Community Growth Rate** — new members per week/month
- **Organic vs. Paid Split** — percentage of new users from organic channels
- **Net Revenue Retention** — expansion revenue from existing customers
