# 03 — Planning: The Blueprint Phase

> You've validated the idea. Now it's time to turn it into an actionable plan. This department is where you define **what** to build, **how** to build it, and **when** to deliver it.

## Purpose

Planning is the bridge between "validated idea" and "shipping product." Skip this, and you'll waste months building features nobody asked for, choosing technologies that don't scale, or missing deadlines because you never set them. Do this well, and your development phase becomes execution instead of exploration.

The biggest planning mistake: over-planning. You're not writing a 50-page PRD. You're making enough decisions to start building with confidence and course-correct as you learn.

## How to Work Through This Department

**Order matters.** Each step builds on the previous:

1. **Product Roadmap** — Define the big picture: what you're building over the next 6-12 months
2. **Feature Prioritization** — Score and rank every feature idea
3. **MVP Scope** — Draw the line: what's in v1 and what's not
4. **Tech Stack** — Choose your technologies based on what you're building
5. **Development Plan** — Break the MVP into sprints and milestones

## Time Estimate

| Approach | Time |
|---|---|
| Solo founder, simple product | 3-5 days |
| Small team, moderate complexity | 1-2 weeks |
| Complex product, multiple stakeholders | 2-4 weeks |

## Key Principles

- **Ship small, learn fast.** Your MVP should take weeks to build, not months.
- **Prioritize ruthlessly.** If everything is P0, nothing is P0.
- **Decide, don't debate.** An imperfect tech stack shipping today beats a perfect one shipping in 6 months.
- **Write it down.** Plans that live in your head don't survive contact with reality.

## What Success Looks Like

When you finish this department, you should have:
- A clear product roadmap with Now/Next/Later priorities
- A scored feature list with objective prioritization
- A defined MVP scope that a developer could start building tomorrow
- Technology choices made with rationale documented
- A sprint plan with specific milestones and deadlines
