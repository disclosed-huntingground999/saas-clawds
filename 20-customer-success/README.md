# 20 — Customer Success: Proactive Retention & Expansion

> Customer support is reactive; Customer Success is proactive. This department covers health scoring, structured onboarding, QBRs, expansion playbooks, and churn prevention — turning customers into advocates and expansion revenue.

## Purpose

Retention (14) covers user onboarding (product UX) and support (reactive). Customer Success is the **proactive** function — identifying at-risk accounts, driving adoption, conducting QBRs, and owning expansion. CS is a growth driver, not a cost center.

| Subfolder | What You'll Produce | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| [health-scoring](./health-scoring/) | Customer health metrics, risk signals, scoring model | P0 |
| [onboarding-program](./onboarding-program/) | Structured onboarding journey for new customers | P0 |
| [quarterly-business-reviews](./quarterly-business-reviews/) | QBR templates, success metrics, expansion signals | P1 |
| [expansion-playbook](./expansion-playbook/) | Upsell/cross-sell motions, champion identification | P1 |
| [churn-prevention](./churn-prevention/) | At-risk intervention, save plays, executive escalation | P0 |

## How to Work Through This Department

1. **Health Scoring** — Define what "healthy" looks like; build risk signals
2. **Onboarding Program** — Structured journey for new customers (post-sale)
3. **Quarterly Business Reviews** — QBR cadence, templates, success metrics
4. **Expansion Playbook** — When and how to upsell; champion identification
5. **Churn Prevention** — At-risk playbook, save offers, escalation

## Time Estimate

| Approach | Time |
|---|---|
| Self-serve / SMB (light CS) | 1–2 weeks |
| Enterprise / high-touch CS | 3–4 weeks |

## Key Principle

**Health before revenue.** Fix at-risk accounts before chasing new logo. A 5% churn reduction can increase profits 25–95%.
