# 08 — Launch: Shipping Your Product to the World

> You've built it. You've tested it. Now it's time to put it in front of real humans and find out if you were right. Launch is where theory meets market reality.

## Purpose

Launching a SaaS isn't a single event — it's a sequence of progressively larger bets. You start with a handful of beta users, graduate to early adopters, build social proof, and then open the floodgates. Each stage teaches you something the previous one couldn't.

Most founders either launch too quietly (building in a vacuum) or too loudly (promising what they can't deliver). This department helps you find the sweet spot: enough exposure to learn fast, enough control to fix what's broken.

## How to Work Through This Department

**Launch in stages, not all at once:**

1. **Landing Page** — Build your storefront before you open for business
2. **Beta Users** — Get your first 10-50 users and learn from them
3. **Early Adopters** — Turn your best beta users into evangelists
4. **Product Hunt** — Execute a coordinated launch for maximum visibility
5. **Public Release** — Open the doors with confidence and a plan

## Time Estimate

| Approach | Time |
|---|---|
| Soft launch (landing page + beta) | 1–2 weeks |
| Coordinated launch (PH + community) | 3–4 weeks of prep |
| Full launch sequence (all stages) | 6–10 weeks |

## Key Principle

**Launch is a process, not a moment.** The best SaaS launches are boring from the outside — a steady drumbeat of beta invites, testimonials, content, and community engagement. The "big bang" Product Hunt launch works best when you've already built momentum.

## What Success Looks Like

When you finish this department, you should have:
- A **landing page** that clearly communicates your value and converts visitors
- **Beta users** who've validated your core features and provided feedback
- **Early adopters** who actively promote your product
- A **Product Hunt launch** (or equivalent) that drives a spike of signups
- A **go-live checklist** ensuring nothing critical was forgotten
