# 14 — Retention

> Keeping customers happy and engaged — because a leaky bucket can never fill up.

Retention is the foundation of sustainable SaaS growth. You can acquire all the customers in the world, but if they churn after 60 days, you're running on a treadmill. Improving retention by even 5% can increase profits by 25-95% (Bain & Company). Every dollar spent on retention returns more than a dollar spent on acquisition.

The goal of this department is to systematically reduce churn, increase engagement, and turn customers into advocates who stay for years and expand their usage over time.

## Why This Matters

SaaS math is brutal. If you have 5% monthly churn, you lose half your customers every year. That means you need to acquire as many new customers each year just to stay flat — before you can even think about growth. Fixing retention is the highest-leverage activity in SaaS after achieving product-market fit.

## How to Work Through This Department

1. **Design user onboarding** — nail the first experience, or nothing else matters
2. **Build email automation** — lifecycle emails keep users engaged between sessions
3. **Set up customer support** — every support interaction is a retention opportunity
4. **Drive feature adoption** — usage of key features is the best predictor of retention
5. **Attack churn directly** — understand why customers leave and build systems to prevent it

## Key Metrics to Track

- **Day 1 / Day 7 / Day 30 Retention** — early retention predicts long-term retention
- **Time to Value** — how fast new users reach their first success
- **Activation Rate** — percentage completing key onboarding milestones
- **NPS (Net Promoter Score)** — overall customer satisfaction and advocacy
- **CSAT** — Customer Satisfaction score for support interactions
- **Monthly/Annual Churn Rate** — both logo churn and revenue churn
- **Net Revenue Retention (NRR)** — target >100% (best-in-class: 120%+)
