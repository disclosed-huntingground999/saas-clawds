# 21 — Product Management: Ongoing Product Discipline

> Planning (03) gave you the initial roadmap. Product Management is the **ongoing discipline** — feedback loops, PMF measurement, release management, roadmap communication, and product ops. This is how product decisions get made after launch.

## Purpose

Product doesn't stop at launch. You need systems to collect feedback, measure product-market fit, ship releases cleanly, communicate the roadmap, and run product operations. This department codifies that discipline.

| Subfolder | What You'll Produce | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| [feedback-loops](./feedback-loops/) | Feature requests, feedback synthesis, voting | P0 |
| [product-market-fit](./product-market-fit/) | PMF surveys, Sean Ellis test, retention signals | P0 |
| [release-management](./release-management/) | Release process, changelog, feature flags | P1 |
| [roadmap-communication](./roadmap-communication/) | Public roadmap, internal alignment | P1 |
| [product-ops](./product-ops/) | Tooling, workflows, rituals | P2 |

## How to Work Through This Department

1. **Feedback Loops** — Capture, prioritize, and close the loop on feedback
2. **Product-Market Fit** — Measure PMF and retention-based signals
3. **Release Management** — Define release process, changelog, rollout
4. **Roadmap Communication** — Internal and external roadmap sharing
5. **Product Ops** — Tooling, rituals, cross-functional workflows

## Time Estimate

| Approach | Time |
|---|---|
| Solo founder (light process) | 1–2 weeks |
| With PM or team | 2–3 weeks |

## Key Principle

**Ship, learn, iterate.** Product management is not about perfection — it's about consistent feedback loops and data-driven decisions.
