# Influencer Outreach — Amplify Reach Through Industry Voices

## Overview

This folder contains your influencer outreach strategy — the plan for leveraging industry influencers, thought leaders, and content creators to amplify your product's reach. In SaaS, "influencer" doesn't mean Instagram celebrities — it means the newsletter writers, YouTubers, podcasters, and Twitter voices your target customers trust and follow.

A single mention from the right person can drive more qualified traffic than months of your own content. The key is building genuine relationships, not transactional one-off promotions.

## Questions to Answer

Before generating the influencer strategy, the founder needs to answer:

1. **Who are the top 20 influencers in your niche?** (Newsletter authors, YouTubers, podcasters, Twitter/LinkedIn voices, bloggers your ICP follows)
2. **What value can you offer influencers?** (Free product access, exclusive features, co-creation opportunities, affiliate commission, cash sponsorship)
3. **What is your budget for sponsored content?** ($0 = relationship-only approach, $500-5,000/mo = paid sponsorships possible)
4. **Micro vs. macro influencer strategy?** (Micro: 1K-50K followers, higher engagement, lower cost. Macro: 50K+, broader reach, higher cost)
5. **What does a successful influencer partnership look like for you?** (Product review? Newsletter mention? Podcast appearance? Co-created content?)
6. **What is your timeline for results?** (Relationship-building takes 3-6 months; paid placements can work in weeks)
7. **Do you have case studies or impressive metrics to share?** (Influencers need proof that the product is worth recommending)

## Output Template

Generate a comprehensive influencer outreach strategy with these sections:

### 1. Influencer Target List
- **Tier 1 (Dream 5):** Top voices in your space — long-term relationship targets
- **Tier 2 (Target 10):** Mid-size influencers — reachable with the right approach
- **Tier 3 (Quick Wins 10-20):** Micro-influencers — easiest to start with
- For each: name, platform, audience size, content type, relevance score (1-5), contact info

### 2. Outreach Email Templates
- **Initial outreach** — warm intro, no ask, genuine compliment + value
- **Follow-up** — share something useful (their audience data, a relevant insight)
- **The ask** — specific, low-friction collaboration proposal
- **Post-collaboration thank you** — nurture the relationship for long-term partnership

### 3. Partnership Structures
- **Free product access** — give them the full product, no strings attached
- **Affiliate deal** — revenue share for signups they drive (20-30% recurring typical)
- **Sponsored content** — paid newsletter mentions, video reviews, podcast ads
- **Co-creation** — joint webinars, co-authored guides, shared research
- **Beta access** — early access to new features in exchange for feedback + coverage

### 4. ROI Tracking
- Unique referral links or codes per influencer
- UTM parameters for all influencer-shared links
- Track: impressions, clicks, signups, conversions, revenue per influencer
- Cost per acquisition calculation per partnership type

### 5. Relationship Management
- CRM or spreadsheet to track all influencer interactions
- Touchpoint cadence (engage with their content weekly, message monthly)
- Milestone celebrations (congratulate them on achievements)
- Annual review: which relationships are worth deepening?

## Agent Instructions

When populating this folder, the AI agent should:

1. Read `company-profile.md` to understand the product niche and target audience
2. Research and list 20 potential influencers across 3 tiers (use the product category to identify relevant voices)
3. Write 4 outreach email templates (intro, follow-up, the ask, thank you)
4. Define 3-4 partnership structures with specific terms (commission rates, deliverables, timelines)
5. Create an ROI tracking framework with attribution methodology
6. Build a 90-day relationship-building timeline
7. Read `09-acquisition/social-media/README.md` to align influencer strategy with social presence
8. Output everything as `README.md` in this folder
