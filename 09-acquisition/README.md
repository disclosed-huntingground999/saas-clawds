# 09 — Acquisition

> Getting users in the door — the top of the funnel.

Acquisition is the growth engine that feeds everything downstream. Without a steady stream of new visitors, signups, and leads, nothing else in your SaaS matters. This department covers every channel you'll use to attract potential customers to your product.

## Why This Matters

Revenue starts here. Every paying customer began as someone who discovered your product through one of these channels. The goal is to build a diversified, repeatable acquisition machine — not depend on a single channel that could dry up overnight.

## Subfolders

| Folder | What It Covers | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| `seo-wins/` | Organic search strategy, keyword targeting, technical SEO | P0 — compounds over time |
| `content-marketing/` | Blog posts, guides, videos, podcasts that attract and educate | P0 |
| `social-media/` | Platform presence where your target customers hang out | P1 |
| `cold-email/` | Outbound email campaigns to reach prospects directly | P1 |
| `influencer-outreach/` | Leveraging industry voices to amplify your reach | P2 |
| `affiliate-marketing/` | Performance-based partner programs | P2 |

## How to Work Through This Department

1. **Start with SEO and content** — these compound over time and take months to pay off, so start early
2. **Layer on social media** — amplify the content you're already creating
3. **Add outbound** — cold email fills pipeline while inbound ramps up
4. **Expand with influencers and affiliates** — these multiply your reach once you have product-market fit

## Key Metrics to Track

- **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** — total spend / new customers acquired, per channel
- **Traffic by source** — organic, direct, referral, social, paid
- **Lead-to-customer conversion rate** — per channel
- **Time to first value** — from first touch to meaningful product usage
- **Channel ROI** — revenue attributed to each channel vs. cost
