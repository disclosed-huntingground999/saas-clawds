# 02 — Validation: Prove Demand Before You Build

> Ideas are cheap. Validated ideas are priceless. This department is about collecting **evidence** that real people will pay for your solution — before you write a single line of code.

## Purpose

The graveyard of failed startups is full of products nobody asked for. Validation is your insurance policy against building the wrong thing. The goal isn't to prove you're right — it's to find out where you're wrong **cheaply and quickly**.

This department gives you five methods to test demand, from conversations to cold hard cash:

| Subfolder | What You'll Produce | Priority | Signal Strength |
|---|---|---|---|
| [customer-interviews](./customer-interviews/) | Interview insights and validated assumptions | P0 | Medium (words) |
| [landing-page-test](./landing-page-test/) | Test page with conversion metrics | P0 | Medium (clicks) |
| [waitlist](./waitlist/) | Pre-launch waitlist with engagement data | P1 | Medium-High (emails) |
| [pre-sales](./pre-sales/) | Revenue before product exists | P1 | Very High (money) |
| [demand-testing](./demand-testing/) | Structured experiment results | P1 | Varies (data) |

## Signal Hierarchy

Not all validation is equal. Ranked from weakest to strongest:

1. **"That sounds cool"** — Worthless. People are polite.
2. **Email signup** — Mild interest. Low commitment.
3. **Time commitment** (30-min interview, detailed survey) — Moderate signal.
4. **Public commitment** (sharing, referring others) — Strong signal.
5. **Money** (pre-order, deposit, annual commitment) — Strongest signal.

## How to Work Through This Department

You don't need to complete every subfolder. Choose based on your stage:

- **Very early (just an idea):** Start with customer interviews + landing page test
- **Some confidence:** Add waitlist + demand testing
- **Ready to commit:** Go for pre-sales

## Time Estimate

| Approach | Time |
|---|---|
| Quick validation (interviews + landing page) | 1–2 weeks |
| Thorough validation (all five methods) | 3–6 weeks |

## Key Principle

**Talk to people before building for people.** Every day spent validating saves a week of building the wrong thing.

## What Success Looks Like

When you finish this department, you should have:
- Direct quotes from potential customers describing the problem in their own words
- Quantitative data showing people will take action (signup, click, share, pay)
- A clear signal on willingness to pay and price sensitivity
- Confidence (or healthy skepticism) about your opportunity thesis
