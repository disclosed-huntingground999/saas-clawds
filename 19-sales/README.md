# 19 — Sales: Close Deals and Grow Pipeline

> For B2B SaaS, sales is the engine that turns pipeline into revenue. This department covers sales process, demo playbooks, proposals, CRM setup, and sales hiring — so you can scale from founder-led sales to a repeatable machine.

## Purpose

Conversion (11) covers the funnel; Sales covers the **human motion** — demos, proposals, negotiation, and closing. Whether you're solo closing or building a team, a clear sales process increases win rates and shortens cycles.

| Subfolder | What You'll Produce | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| [sales-process](./sales-process/) | Methodology, pipeline stages, qualification | P0 |
| [demo-playbook](./demo-playbook/) | Demo scripts, objection handling, competitive positioning | P0 |
| [proposals-and-contracts](./proposals-and-contracts/) | Proposal templates, SOW, pricing quotes | P1 |
| [crm-setup](./crm-setup/) | CRM configuration, pipeline, reporting | P1 |
| [sales-hiring](./sales-hiring/) | Roles, comp plans, ramp, territories | P2 |

## How to Work Through This Department

1. **Sales Process** — Define stages, qualification, and methodology
2. **Demo Playbook** — Script demos, handle objections, position vs competitors
3. **Proposals and Contracts** — Create templates for proposals and SOWs
4. **CRM Setup** — Configure pipeline, fields, and reporting
5. **Sales Hiring** — When and how to hire your first salespeople

## Time Estimate

| Approach | Time |
|---|---|
| Founder-led sales (process + demo + CRM) | 1–2 weeks |
| First sales hire (add comp plan, territory) | 2–3 weeks |

## Key Principle

**Process before people.** Document your sales process before hiring. Founders who close the first 10–20 deals understand the playbook; codify it so it's repeatable.
