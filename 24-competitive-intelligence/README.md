# 24 — Competitive Intelligence: Stay Ahead of the Market

> Idea (01) gave you a one-time competitor analysis. Competitive Intelligence is the **ongoing discipline** — market monitoring, win/loss analysis, feature gap tracking, pricing intelligence, and battlecards. Stay informed and outmaneuver competitors.

## Purpose

Competitive advantage erodes fast. Ongoing competitive intel — win/loss interviews, feature tracking, pricing changes, and sales battlecards — keeps your team informed and your positioning sharp.

| Subfolder | What You'll Produce | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| [market-monitoring](./market-monitoring/) | Ongoing competitive tracking, alerts | P1 |
| [win-loss-analysis](./win-loss-analysis/) | Post-deal interviews, win/loss patterns | P0 |
| [feature-gap-tracking](./feature-gap-tracking/) | Feature parity matrix, gap prioritization | P1 |
| [pricing-intelligence](./pricing-intelligence/) | Competitor pricing, packaging trends | P1 |
| [battlecards](./battlecards/) | Sales battlecards per competitor | P0 |

## How to Work Through This Department

1. **Market Monitoring** — Set up ongoing competitive tracking
2. **Win/Loss Analysis** — Interview won and lost deals; find patterns
3. **Feature Gap Tracking** — Track competitor features; prioritize gaps
4. **Pricing Intelligence** — Monitor competitor pricing and packaging
5. **Battlecards** — Create sales battlecards for top competitors

## Time Estimate

| Approach | Time |
|---|---|
| Light (battlecards + win/loss) | 1–2 weeks |
| Full (all subfolders) | 2–4 weeks |

## Key Principle

**Intel without action is wasted.** Connect competitive intel to product (feature gaps), sales (battlecards), and marketing (positioning).
