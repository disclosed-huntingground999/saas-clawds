# 22 — Brand & Positioning: Stand Out and Be Remembered

> Design (04) covers the product UI. Brand & Positioning covers your **company identity** — name, logo, messaging, voice, and competitive positioning. A strong brand increases recognition, trust, and conversion.

## Purpose

Brand is how you're perceived. Positioning is how you're differentiated. This department gives you the frameworks and assets to build a memorable brand that resonates with your target customer and stands apart from competitors.

| Subfolder | What You'll Produce | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| [brand-identity](./brand-identity/) | Name, logo, visual identity, guidelines | P0 |
| [messaging-framework](./messaging-framework/) | Value props, taglines, positioning statement | P0 |
| [voice-and-tone](./voice-and-tone/) | Writing style guide, tone by channel | P1 |
| [competitive-positioning](./competitive-positioning/) | Positioning map, differentiation matrix | P1 |
| [brand-assets](./brand-assets/) | Logo files, templates, brand kit | P1 |

## How to Work Through This Department

1. **Brand Identity** — Name, logo, colors, typography, visual system
2. **Messaging Framework** — Value props, positioning, elevator pitch
3. **Voice and Tone** — How you write; tone by channel
4. **Competitive Positioning** — How you're different; battlecards
5. **Brand Assets** — Logo files, social templates, slide deck

## Time Estimate

| Approach | Time |
|---|---|
| DIY (founder + tools) | 1–2 weeks |
| With designer / agency | 2–4 weeks |

## Key Principle

**Consistency builds recognition.** Use the same voice, visuals, and messaging everywhere. Inconsistency dilutes brand.
