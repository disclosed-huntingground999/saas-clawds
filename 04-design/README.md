# 04 — Design: Make It Beautiful, Usable, and Consistent

> Great SaaS products don't just work — they **feel** right. This department is about designing a product that's intuitive, visually polished, and consistent across every screen.

## Purpose

Design isn't a luxury for later. It's a competitive advantage from Day 1. Users form opinions about your product in the first 5 seconds. A well-designed MVP signals credibility, reduces support burden, and increases conversion at every stage of the funnel.

That said, design at the MVP stage isn't about pixel perfection. It's about **clarity**: can the user understand what to do, do it quickly, and feel good about the experience?

This department walks you through the design process from rough sketches to a scalable design system:

| Subfolder | What You'll Produce | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| [wireframes](./wireframes/) | Low-fidelity layout sketches for all key screens | P0 |
| [ui-design](./ui-design/) | High-fidelity visual designs | P1 |
| [ux-flows](./ux-flows/) | User journey maps through core workflows | P0 |
| [prototype](./prototype/) | Clickable prototype for user testing | P1 |
| [design-system](./design-system/) | Reusable components, tokens, and guidelines | P2 |

## How to Work Through This Department

**Start with flows, then wireframes, then visuals:**

1. **UX Flows** — Map the user journeys before designing any screens
2. **Wireframes** — Sketch layout and information hierarchy (no colors, no polish)
3. **UI Design** — Apply visual design, branding, and polish
4. **Prototype** — Make it clickable and test with real users
5. **Design System** — Extract reusable patterns as you go

## Time Estimate

| Approach | Time |
|---|---|
| Founder-led (using templates, AI tools) | 3-5 days |
| Designer on team | 1-2 weeks |
| Outsourced design | 2-4 weeks |

## Key Principles

- **Function before form.** Ugly but usable beats beautiful but confusing.
- **Steal like an artist.** Study SaaS products you admire. Screenshot their best patterns.
- **Consistency is trust.** Use the same patterns, spacing, and colors everywhere.
- **Mobile can wait (usually).** Most B2B SaaS is desktop-first. Design for your primary device.
- **Ship imperfect.** You'll iterate the design after watching real users struggle.

## What Success Looks Like

When you finish this department, you should have:
- User flows mapping every core journey through your product
- Wireframes for every screen in your MVP
- Visual designs (at least for core screens) with your brand applied
- A clickable prototype you can test with real users
- The beginnings of a design system you can scale
