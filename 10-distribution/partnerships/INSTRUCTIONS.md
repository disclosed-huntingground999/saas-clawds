# Partnerships — Strategic Alliances for Mutual Distribution

## Overview

This folder contains your partnership strategy — the plan for forming strategic alliances with complementary companies to expand distribution for both parties. Partnerships are a multiplier: you get access to an established audience, and your partner gets access to yours.

The best SaaS partnerships aren't just "let's put each other's logos on our websites." They involve real co-marketing, co-selling, product integration, or audience sharing that creates genuine value for both sides' customers.

## Questions to Answer

Before generating the partnership strategy, the founder needs to answer:

1. **What complementary products do your customers already use?** (Products that solve adjacent problems — not competitors, but complements)
2. **What co-marketing opportunities exist?** (Joint webinars, co-authored content, shared email sends, bundle offers)
3. **What integration partnerships make sense?** (Products you could build deep integrations with, appearing in each other's ecosystems)
4. **Would referral agreements work?** (Formal referral fees when one partner sends a customer to the other)
5. **Are there channel partners or resellers?** (Agencies, consultants, or VARs who could sell your product to their clients)
6. **What does a dream partnership look like?** (If you could partner with any company, who and why?)
7. **What value do you bring to a partner?** (Why would they say yes? What's in it for them?)

## Output Template

Generate a comprehensive partnership strategy with these sections:

### 1. Partner Landscape Map
- **Complementary SaaS tools** — products your customers use alongside yours
- **Agencies and consultants** — service providers who serve your target customer
- **Industry platforms** — larger platforms where you could become a preferred app
- **Content partners** — media, newsletters, communities with your target audience
- **Technology partners** — infrastructure or platform providers

### 2. Partner Evaluation Matrix
| Partner | Type | Their Audience | Overlap | Effort | Potential Value | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Company A] | Integration | 50K users | High | Medium | Revenue share + co-marketing | P0 |
| [Company B] | Co-marketing | 20K newsletter | High | Low | Content swap + leads | P1 |
| [Agency C] | Channel | 200 clients | Medium | Low | Referral fees | P1 |

### 3. Partnership Proposal Template
- Opening: why this partnership makes sense for both parties
- Value exchange: what each side contributes and receives
- Specific partnership type: integration, co-marketing, referral, reseller
- Metrics: how you'll measure success
- Timeline: phased approach starting small
- Next steps: specific ask

### 4. Co-Marketing Plan
- **Joint webinars:** quarterly webinars on shared audience topics
- **Co-authored content:** guides, reports, or case studies
- **Email swaps:** featured placement in each other's newsletters
- **Social amplification:** cross-promote each other's content
- **Bundle offers:** special pricing for customers of both products

### 5. Partnership Tiers
| Tier | Commitment | Benefits | Requirements |
|---|---|---|---|
| Referral | Low — share referral links | 15-20% referral fee | Signed referral agreement |
| Co-marketing | Medium — joint content quarterly | Shared leads + brand exposure | Quarterly planning call |
| Integration | High — build product integration | Marketplace listing + co-sell | Engineering resources + support |
| Strategic | Highest — deep alignment | Revenue share + executive sponsorship | Formal partnership agreement |

### 6. Partner Relationship Management
- Partner onboarding process
- Quarterly business review template
- Communication cadence (monthly check-ins, quarterly reviews)
- Partner portal or shared workspace setup
- Success metrics and reporting
