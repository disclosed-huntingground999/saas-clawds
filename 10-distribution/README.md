# 10 — Distribution

> Be everywhere your target customers look.

Distribution is about making your product discoverable through multiple channels. While acquisition is about actively reaching out, distribution is about being found — showing up in the directories, marketplaces, communities, and ecosystems where your ideal customers already spend their time.

## Why This Matters

The best product with zero distribution loses to a mediocre product with great distribution. Most SaaS founders underinvest here. Listing your product in the right places creates passive, compounding discovery that works 24/7 without ongoing effort.

## Subfolders

| Folder | What It Covers | Priority |
|---|---|---|
| `directories/` | SaaS directories and review sites (G2, Capterra, etc.) | P0 — high leverage, low effort |
| `saas-marketplaces/` | App stores and marketplace ecosystems | P1 |
| `communities/` | Online communities where customers congregate | P0 |
| `partnerships/` | Strategic partnerships for mutual distribution | P1 |
| `integrations/` | Building into users' existing tool ecosystems | P1 |

## How to Work Through This Department

1. **List on directories immediately** — it's free, fast, and compounds with reviews over time
2. **Engage in communities** — show up where your customers already are (give value first)
3. **Evaluate marketplace fit** — if your product fits a major ecosystem, this can be a top channel
4. **Build strategic partnerships** — find complementary products, not competitors
5. **Plan integration strategy** — integrations make your product stickier and more discoverable

## Key Metrics to Track

- **Referral traffic by source** — which directories and communities drive actual signups
- **Marketplace installs/signups** — volume and conversion from each marketplace
- **Partnership-sourced revenue** — revenue attributed to partner channels
- **Integration adoption rate** — percentage of users connecting integrations
- **Directory review score** — average rating across listing sites
